Goal setting#

As stated in my last blog post, the goals for the week are the following:

“I’ll go with 12 theorems and 9 groups of definitions, aka until lesson 26th of my textbook (note to future self).

In terms of exercises, I want to reach an “almost ready for the exam” level in limits, sequences, and series. I’ll also practice some derivatives, even if I’ll only find them in the study of functions.

I’ll go soft on myself about Japanese because I sometimes get creative in how I interact with it. I’m starting to look at accurate and less interpretative translations of Japanese songs. So I’ll just finish the first 2 chapters of Japanese from zero 1 and get confident about my hiragana knowledge.”

Let’s get started BD

Monday#

I’m not gonna lie, I woke up almost 3 hours ago because I stayed in bed after I woke up, I finished writing the week 0 blog post and I had to debug why my website wasn’t working (thanks Github workflows).

My goals for today are:

  • Following my spaced repetition schedule
  • Studying 4 groups of definitions and the Bolzano-Weierstrass theorem and demostration.
  • Finishing Elia Bombardelli’s YouTube series about limits
  • Try out previous exams’ exercises about limits
  • Fulling the hiragana quiz at least once
  • Finishing the Japanese from Zero pre-lessons and starting chapter 1

Holy moly, let’s get started! P.S. It’s currently 9:15 AM.

2 PM#

This morning was spectacular, I absolutely ate those exams exercises, I just have to polish how to show and prove my results. I’ll do it some days prior to the exam, the most important thing is getting to the right results in all the various kinds of exercises.

I was a bit early on schedule and definitely slacked. I didn’t really study the theory but I’ve read it, understood it and I’m sure that when I’ll be back I’ll study it in a breeze.

I’ve also noticed that there are other theorems before the Bolzano-Weierstrass one. I’m not gonna explain the whole thing, but they’re less likely to be found in the exam demonstrations lottery üòé

One of them is absoulely outrageous, so I’ll just study the statement and pray for good luck. I’ll study the other two, and leave the Bolzano-Weierstrass one for tomorrow.

Now I’ll go buy some soju in the multiethnical neighborhood - we’ll drink it for new year’s eve -, go to the hospital for an exciting but redacted event, and be back for studying in 4 or 5 hours. Poor Japanese… it’s waiting for me. Cya!
